For years, PlayStation owners have watched with a mix of envy and anticipation as Bethesda's sprawling space epic, Starfield, remained an Xbox and PC exclusive. That wait is finally over, with tantalising new reports suggesting that PS5 pre-orders are set to open as early as March 18, 2026, ahead of a physical release on April 7, 2026. This is huge news for UK console gamers, opening up one of the biggest RPGs of recent years to a whole new audience and potentially sparking some interesting price movements across the [gaming hardware](https://ctrlaltstock.com/blog/article-rtx4090-amd-discussion) and software landscape.
## What's the Story?
The gaming world has been abuzz since reports from French deals site Dealabs, known for its remarkably reliable leaks regarding gaming releases and deals, indicated that Starfield is indeed heading to the PlayStation 5. According to their sources, PlayStation owners can mark their calendars for March 18, 2026, when digital pre-orders for the highly anticipated title are expected to go live. There's even a chance they could drop on the evening of March 17th, so keep your eyes peeled across the PlayStation Store! For those who prefer a physical copy to proudly display on their shelf – a cherished tradition for many collectors – the boxed edition is slated for release a few weeks later, on April 7, 2026.
This announcement marks a significant shift in strategy for Microsoft and Bethesda. Starfield, developed by the legendary studio behind The Elder Scrolls and Fallout, was initially released in September 2023 as a flagship exclusive for Xbox Series X/S and PC, following Microsoft's acquisition of Bethesda's parent company, ZeniMax Media, for a staggering $7.5 billion in 2021. The game quickly garnered attention for its vast open-world (or rather, open-galaxy) exploration, deep character customisation, and classic Bethesda RPG mechanics, allowing players to traverse over a thousand planets, engage in space combat, and delve into a compelling main narrative alongside countless side quests. Its console exclusivity was a major selling point for the Xbox ecosystem, and its arrival on PlayStation 5 represents a major broadening of its audience, bringing the epic sci-fi adventure to millions of new players.
While specific details regarding any PS5-exclusive content or enhancements are yet to be revealed, it's widely expected that the PlayStation version will offer the same core experience enjoyed by Xbox and PC players, potentially with optimisations for the PS5's unique features like the DualSense controller's haptic feedback and adaptive triggers. This move aligns with a broader industry trend of former exclusives making their way to other platforms, allowing publishers to tap into larger player bases and extend the lifespan of their titles. Recent examples include Xbox-published titles like *Hi-Fi Rush*, *Grounded*, *Pentiment*, and *Sea of Thieves* all making their way to PlayStation or [Nintendo Switch](https://ctrlaltstock.com/blog/avatar-last-airbender-game-9-99-amazon), demonstrating a strategic pivot towards multi-platform releases for many studios. For PlayStation enthusiasts in the CtrlAltStock community who might have felt left out, this is a moment to celebrate – the stars are finally within reach on your preferred console.

*   **Launch Price:** Expect Starfield on PS5 to launch at the standard full-price for a new AAA title in the UK. This typically falls between **£60 and £70** for the standard digital or physical edition. Retailers like GAME, Amazon UK, Currys, Argos, and ShopTo will likely list it at or around **£69.99**. Given its status as a major release, don't expect deep discounts right out of the gate, especially for pre-orders.
*   **Premium/Deluxe Editions:** Like many modern games, Starfield on Xbox and PC offered Premium Editions with early access and cosmetic bonuses. It's highly probable that a similar structure will be available for the PS5 version. The Starfield Premium Edition on other platforms included the base game, the "Shattered Space" story expansion (when released), a Constellation Skin Pack (Laser Rifle, Spacesuit, Helmet, and Boost Pack), and access to a Digital Artbook and Original Soundtrack. These premium versions are likely to be priced upwards of **£80-£100**, perhaps around **£89.99** or **£99.99**, offering extra content for dedicated fans.
