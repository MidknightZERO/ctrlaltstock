**H2: What's the Story?**
The core news originates from a thread on the gaming forum Resetera, where Jason Schreier, a respected industry reporter for Bloomberg News, shared a direct quote from Sony. The quote, attributed to an unnamed Sony executive, stated: *"PlayStation's Santa Monica Studio is developing a new franchise within the God of War universe: I suppose now it's become obvious what I meant by 'not a new IP but it might feel like one.'"* This cryptic comment, made months prior during discussions about the studio's future, has now been clarified. Santa Monica Studio, the acclaimed developers behind the 2018 *God of War* reboot, *God of War: Ragnarök* (2022), and supporting the 2024 *Ragnarök* PC port, is not creating a direct sequel to the current Norse era saga. Instead, they are spearheading a **new, distinct franchise** set within the established God of War universe. This means fans can expect a fresh story, potentially new protagonists or a focus on different mythological pantheons (such as Egyptian or Mesopotamian, long-rumoured), and a potentially different tone or gameplay focus, while still being deeply rooted in the lore, locations, and foundational themes Kratos and Atreus explored. The exact nature of this new franchise – whether it's a prequel exploring Kratos's earlier years in Greece, a spin-off set in a completely different era with new characters, or a completely original story using the universe as a backdrop – remains under wraps. What is clear is that Santa Monica Studio is leveraging their deep knowledge of the franchise's mechanics and narrative weight to create something new, aiming for a feel that resonates with the God of War spirit without being a direct continuation. This development was first reported by Schreier on his *On Sports* Substack and later covered by major outlets like IGN and GamesRadar+. Industry analysts note this follows a broader Sony strategy of expanding its most valuable first-party IPs into multi-franchise ecosystems, akin to how *The Last of Us* now includes the acclaimed TV series and remake projects, or how *Horizon* is branching out.
**H2: What Does This Mean for Prices?**
The immediate impact on PS5 hardware and game prices is likely to be indirect but potentially significant for bargain hunters in the UK. First, the announcement fuels continued, steady demand for the PS5 console itself. While the base PS5 (with disc drive) and PS5 Digital Edition have seen price stability in the UK market—officially retailing at £449.99 and £349.99 respectively since 2023—the anticipation surrounding new, major God of War content could drive interest in console bundles or used PS5 systems as fans look to ensure they can play future titles. Retailers like Currys PC World, Argos, and Amazon UK often create limited-time bundles pairing the console with popular games like *God of War: Ragnarök* at a slight discount (e.g., a PS5 Slim bundle with *Ragnarök* listed at £479.99 versus buying separately). The news may subtly increase the desirability of such bundles. Second, the news impacts the used game market. *God of War: Ragnarök* remains a highly sought-after title. Its continued presence on the used market, often selling for £25-£35 at CeX or on platforms like eBay, is likely to persist as gamers await the new franchise. However, the announcement might slightly increase the perceived long-term value of *Ragnarök* as a foundational piece of the expanding universe, though this effect is likely modest and slow to materialise. Crucially, **this news does not signal an immediate, dramatic [price drop](https://ctrlaltstock.com/blog/article-rtx4090-amd-discussion) on PS5 consoles or *Ragnarök*.** PS5 prices in the UK have stabilised post-chip shortage, and Sony typically doesn't discount consoles heavily based on future software announcements alone.
