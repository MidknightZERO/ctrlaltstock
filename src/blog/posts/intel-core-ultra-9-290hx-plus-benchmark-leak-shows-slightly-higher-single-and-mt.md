### Understanding the Leaked Processor: Intel Core Ultra 9 290HX Plus
The Intel Core Ultra 9 290HX Plus is rumoured to be a high-performance mobile processor, likely representing the pinnacle of Intel's Arrow Lake-HX refresh or a subsequent generation designed for the most demanding laptop users. The "HX" designation in Intel's mobile processor nomenclature typically indicates a high-performance, unlocked chip with a higher Thermal Design Power (TDP), often exceeding 55W (base power) and capable of boosting to 157W or even higher under peak loads. This makes it suitable for desktop-replacement laptops and high-end gaming machines that can accommodate robust cooling solutions. The "Plus" suffix, while not explicitly defined by Intel yet, could signify a higher-binned version of the 290HX, perhaps with slightly higher clock speeds, more active cores, or better power efficiency characteristics, reserved for the absolute top-tier laptop models.
Building upon the foundations of Intel's 'Core Ultra' branding, we can expect the 290HX Plus to incorporate a sophisticated hybrid architecture, likely featuring a mix of Performance-cores (P-cores) and Efficient-cores (E-cores), along with low-power Efficient-cores (LP E-cores) for background tasks and improved battery life, if it maintains the disaggregated tile design of Meteor Lake. While specific core counts for the 290HX Plus are still speculative, a top-tier HX part would likely feature a significant number, potentially 8 P-cores and up to 16 E-cores, similar to or exceeding the current Core Ultra 9 185HX's 6 P-cores, 16 E-cores, and 2 LP E-cores configuration. Clock speeds would also be pushed to their limits, with boost frequencies potentially reaching well over 5.5 GHz for P-cores, which is crucial for gaming and single-threaded application performance.
Cache sizes are also expected to be generous, with large L2 and shared L3 caches to minimise latency and improve data access speed for demanding applications. For instance, the current Core Ultra 9 185HX boasts 24MB of L3 cache, and a successor would likely offer similar or increased amounts.
A key feature of the Core Ultra series is the integrated Neural Processing Unit (NPU), designed to accelerate AI workloads directly on the device. The 290HX Plus would undoubtedly feature an enhanced NPU, offering significantly more AI TOPS (Trillions of Operations Per Second) than its predecessors. This is increasingly vital for modern applications, powering features like real-time background blur and eye-contact correction in video calls, AI-driven content creation tools, local large language model (LLM) inference, and intelligent power management, all without relying heavily on cloud resources or the GPU.
