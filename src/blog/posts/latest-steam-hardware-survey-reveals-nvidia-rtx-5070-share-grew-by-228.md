The latest Steam Hardware Survey for February 2026 has dropped, and it’s revealing some fascinating shifts in the PC gaming landscape. For bargain hunters and those planning their next build, these trends are crucial. A sudden and dramatic surge in popularity for NVIDIA's GeForce RTX 5070 and RTX 5060 GPUs, alongside a significant jump for 32GB of system RAM, signals changing priorities and potential opportunities in the market. If you're eyeing a new [graphics card](https://ctrlaltstock.com/blog/graphics-card-review-2026) or memory upgrade, understanding these movements could save you a significant chunk of change. The Steam Hardware Survey is a unique and invaluable resource for the PC gaming community. Unlike sales reports that only track units sold, this monthly survey directly polls millions of active Steam users about the hardware configuration of their gaming PCs. This provides a real-time, ground-level view of what components gamers are actually using, offering unparalleled insight into market penetration and prevailing trends. For instance, while a new GPU might sell well, its true adoption rate among the wider gaming population is best reflected here.
## What's the Story?
The big news from the February 2026 Steam Hardware Survey, as reported by WCCFTech, is the astonishing rise of the [NVIDIA GeForce](https://ctrlaltstock.com/blog/micron-gddr7-memory-finally-spotted-on-a-geforce-rtx-50-card-bringing-the-series) RTX 5070. This GPU, which held less than a 3% share in January, exploded to a whopping 9.42% in just one month – a staggering 228% increase in popularity. This propelled it past the long-reigning GeForce RTX 4060 to become the most popular graphics card among Steam users. The RTX 5070, launched in late 2025, quickly established itself as a mid-to-high-range powerhouse, typically offering performance comparable to or exceeding the previous generation's RTX 4070 Ti SUPER, but often at a more accessible price point. It usually comes equipped with 12GB or 16GB of GDDR6X VRAM, making it well-suited for high-refresh-rate 1440p gaming and even entry-level 4K experiences. At launch, its Founders Edition was priced around £589, with custom AIB models from manufacturers like ASUS, MSI, and Gigabyte ranging from £579 to £680 depending on cooling and factory overclocks.
It's not just the RTX 5070 making waves; its more affordable sibling, the GeForce RTX 5060, also saw a substantial increase in its share, solidifying the 50-series as the new darlings of the gaming community. The RTX 5060, often featuring 8GB or 12GB of GDDR6 VRAM, targets the mainstream 1080p and 1440p gaming market, offering a significant performance uplift over its predecessor, the RTX 4060, while maintaining an attractive price-to-performance ratio. Initial retail prices for the RTX 5060 hovered around £369-£420. This rapid adoption suggests that NVIDIA has hit a sweet spot with these new generation cards, offering a compelling blend of performance and, crucially, perceived value for money that resonates with a broad base of gamers. The efficiency improvements and enhanced AI capabilities, including DLSS 4.0, also played a crucial role in their appeal, providing tangible benefits in modern demanding titles like Cyberpunk 2077, Alan Wake 2, and Starfield.
The significance of the Steam Hardware Survey cannot be overstated. Unlike sales figures that only show what people *buy*, this survey shows what hardware people are *using* to game. It’s a powerful snapshot of real-world adoption and indicates which components are truly making their way into gaming PCs. The fact that the RTX 4060's share didn't necessarily decline, but was simply outpaced by the explosive growth of the 50-series, highlights a rapid transition in the market. Gamers aren't just upgrading; they're upgrading to the newest generation in droves, indicating strong confidence in NVIDIA's latest offerings. This rapid shift also implies that many gamers might have been holding out for the next generation, bypassing the RTX 40-series in anticipation of better value and performance.
Beyond GPUs, the survey also highlighted another interesting trend: a sudden surge in the popularity of systems equipped with 32GB of RAM. While 16GB has been the standard for years, the increasing demands of modern games, multitasking, and other applications mean that 32GB is quickly becoming the new baseline for a smooth gaming experience. Many new AAA titles released in 2025 and early 2026, such as "The Elder Scrolls VI" (early access) and "Grand Theft Auto VI", listed 32GB as their recommended system memory, pushing gamers to upgrade. Even with older titles, running background applications like Discord, web browsers with multiple tabs, streaming software, or video editing tools simultaneously can quickly max out 16GB. This suggests a broader trend of gamers future-proofing their systems, ensuring they have enough memory to handle upcoming titles and more intensive workloads, particularly those utilising DDR5 memory platforms that offer higher speeds and capacities. A popular choice for 32GB kits includes 2x16GB modules of DDR4-3600MHz CL16 or DDR5-6000MHz CL30.
