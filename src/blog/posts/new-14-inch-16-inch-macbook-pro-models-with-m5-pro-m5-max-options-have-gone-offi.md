## What's the Story?
Apple, on 3rd March 2026, officially pulled back the curtain on its updated MacBook Pro lineup, featuring the highly anticipated M5 Pro and M5 Max System-on-Chips (SoCs). While the external design remains familiar, retaining the sleek aluminium chassis and robust build quality we've come to expect, the significant upgrades are all under the bonnet. These new machines promise substantial performance and efficiency uplifts, making them incredibly compelling for professionals, power users, and anyone demanding the absolute best from their portable workstation.
The M5 Pro chip, for instance, is expected to feature a 14-core CPU (10 performance cores, 4 efficiency cores) and up to a 20-core GPU, alongside a 32-core Neural Engine for accelerated machine learning tasks. It supports up to 36GB of unified memory with a bandwidth of 200GB/s. The M5 Max takes this even further, boasting a 16-core CPU (12 performance, 4 efficiency) and a staggering 40-core GPU, with a 32-core Neural Engine. It supports up to 96GB of unified memory, offering an incredible 400GB/s of memory bandwidth. These specifications translate into real-world benefits: video editors can expect to render complex 4K and 8K timelines in Final Cut Pro up to 30% faster than M4 models, while 3D artists using applications like Blender or Cinema 4D will see significant reductions in render times. Software developers compiling large codebases will also experience a noticeable boost in productivity.
One of the most welcome changes across the board is the move to 1TB of storage as standard, a much-needed upgrade that provides more breathing room for large projects, applications, and media files right out of the box. This is a considerable improvement over previous base configurations, which often started at 512GB, and reflects the growing demand for higher storage capacities in today's digital landscape. Furthermore, Apple has pushed the boundaries of flash storage performance, with the new SSDs boasting "unworldly speeds" of up to 8GB/s sequential read and write, ensuring lightning-fast file transfers, application launches, and project loading times. Users can configure these machines with up to a staggering 8TB of unified storage, catering to even the most demanding workflows involving massive video libraries or complex 3D assets. For professional photographers working with RAW files or filmmakers managing ProRes footage, this high-speed, high-capacity storage is a game-changer, minimising bottlenecks and maximising creative flow. The base 1TB configuration for the 14-inch M5 Pro model is anticipated to start from around £2,199, while the 16-inch M5 Pro could begin at approximately £2,699. Opting for higher storage tiers, such as 4TB or 8TB, will naturally increase the price significantly, potentially adding £1,000-£2,500 to the base cost depending on the exact configuration.
Beyond the raw processing power, Apple has integrated its new N1 wireless chip, which first debuted in the iPhone 17 series. This advanced chip is expected to deliver enhanced Wi-Fi 7 and Bluetooth 5.4 performance, offering faster, more reliable connections with significantly lower latency – a critical factor for anyone working with cloud-based workflows, large file transfers, or connecting multiple wireless peripherals. Wi-Fi 7, also known as 802.11be, brings substantial speed improvements and better efficiency in congested networks, perfect for busy creative studios or home offices. It supports Multi-Link Operation (MLO), allowing devices to simultaneously send and receive data over multiple frequency bands (2.4GHz, 5GHz, and 6GHz), which dramatically boosts throughput and reduces latency. Theoretically, Wi-Fi 7 can achieve speeds of up to 40Gbps, providing a future-proof connection for years to come, assuming you have a compatible Wi-Fi 7 router. Bluetooth 5.4, on the other hand, introduces LE Audio with Auracast, enabling broadcast audio to multiple devices simultaneously, and further enhances connection reliability and power efficiency for accessories like AirPods Pro or Magic Keyboards. Coupled with this, the new MacBook Pros boast an impressive battery life of up to 24 hours on the 16-inch model, and up to 18 hours on the 14-inch model, setting a new benchmark for endurance in high-performance laptops. Imagine a full day of intensive video editing, coding, or entertainment without needing to hunt for a power outlet, courtesy of the M5 chips' incredible power efficiency and the larger battery capacities of the Pro models. This extended endurance is particularly beneficial for professionals who frequently travel or work remotely without consistent access to power.

These displays offer incredible visual fidelity, with 1,600 nits of peak HDR brightness and 1,000 nits of sustained full-screen brightness, providing truly vibrant and lifelike colours. The extreme dynamic range and a phenomenal 1,000,000:1 contrast ratio make blacks incredibly deep and whites exceptionally bright, ideal for HDR content creation and consumption. Both panels also feature ProMotion technology, offering adaptive refresh rates up to 120Hz, which makes scrolling, gaming, and video playback feel incredibly fluid and responsive. For creative professionals, the displays cover the P3 wide colour gamut, ensuring colour accuracy crucial for graphic design, photography, and video editing.
