PC gamers, listen up! If you've been eyeing the upcoming releases of Kojima Productions' highly anticipated *Death Stranding 2: On the Beach* or Bungie's intense extraction shooter *Marathon*, we've got some fantastic news that could seriously impact your gaming experience and potentially extend the life of your current [graphics card](https://ctrlaltstock.com/blog/graphics-card-review-2026). NVIDIA has just confirmed that its cutting-edge DLSS 4.5 Super Resolution technology will be available for both titles via an app override, promising unparalleled visuals and performance. This is a a genuine game-changer for anyone looking to squeeze every frame out of their NVIDIA RTX hardware, especially those with GeForce RTX 20 Series or 30 Series GPUs, without breaking the bank on an immediate upgrade. It underscores the growing importance of AI-powered upscaling in modern gaming, allowing more players to enjoy high fidelity graphics at smooth frame rates, even on older, yet still capable, hardware.
## What's the Story?
Today, NVIDIA dropped a significant announcement for PC gamers eagerly awaiting two of 2026's biggest titles: *Death Stranding 2: On the Beach* and *Marathon*. Both games, set for release this month, will support the latest iteration of NVIDIA's acclaimed AI-powered upscaling technology, DLSS 4.5 Super Resolution. What makes this particularly exciting is the "NVIDIA app override" mechanism, a new, powerful feature within the recently launched NVIDIA app (which has evolved significantly from the legacy GeForce Experience application). This new app allows GeForce RTX owners to activate DLSS 4.5 Super Resolution manually, potentially before official in-game integration. This driver-level injection offers immediate benefits, providing unparalleled flexibility and ensuring players can access the latest enhancements without waiting for game patches, which can sometimes take weeks or even months to arrive. Users can simply open the NVIDIA app, navigate to the game settings, and force-enable DLSS 4.5 Super Resolution, overriding the game's default or older DLSS version.
Kojima Productions had previously confirmed that *Death Stranding 2: On the Beach* would ship with support for NVIDIA DLSS 4, including its Multi Frame Generation feature, alongside AMD FSR 4 and Intel XeSS 2. Now, the confirmation of DLSS 4.5 Super Resolution via the NVIDIA app means players can opt for the very latest in upscaling quality, right from day one. This is a big deal because DLSS 4.5 recently made headlines after a rigorous blind test conducted by the reputable German tech publication Computerbase. In these tests, DLSS 4.5 reportedly beat AMD FSR 4 and, remarkably, even native resolution rendering in visual fidelity across several scenarios. The test specifically highlighted DLSS 4.5's superior image reconstruction, particularly its ability to drastically reduce ghosting artifacts around fast-moving objects and significantly improve temporal stability in complex scenes, such as dense foliage, intricate particle effects, or fine wireframes. This isn't just about achieving more frames; it's about attaining a superior image quality that can sometimes even surpass what your GPU renders natively, thanks to the power of AI filling in details that traditional rendering might miss or process less optimally, leading to a crisper, more stable image. A dedicated Game Ready driver will also be released closer to *Death Stranding 2*'s launch to ensure optimal performance and stability, working in tandem with the app override for the ultimate visual experience. This driver will be available for download through the NVIDIA app or NVIDIA's website, typically a week or two before the game's release date.
Bungie's *Marathon*, a highly anticipated first-person extraction shooter, is also confirmed to benefit from DLSS 4.5 Super Resolution via the same app override. However, in *Marathon*'s case, it will not support Frame Generation. This is a deliberate and understandable choice, likely made by Bungie to avoid the potential for increased input latency that Frame Generation can introduce, which is a critical consideration in fast-paced, competitive games where every millisecond counts. Frame Generation works by inserting AI-generated frames *between* traditionally rendered frames, inherently adding a slight delay before the first generated frame is displayed to the player. For such titles, particularly those from a developer with Bungie's pedigree in competitive online multiplayer, pure Super Resolution without Frame Generation is often preferred to maintain the lowest possible latency while still boosting frame rates significantly. For example, a player using a GeForce RTX 3070 might see their frame rate jump from 60 FPS to 100 FPS with DLSS 4.5 Super Resolution alone, without the added latency penalty of Frame Generation. The ability to manually enable DLSS 4.5 ensures that *Marathon* players get the best possible visual clarity and performance without compromising responsiveness, making it ideal for competitive play where precision and immediate feedback are paramount.
For those unfamiliar, DLSS (Deep Learning Super Sampling) is NVIDIA's proprietary technology that uses advanced AI algorithms and specialised Tensor Cores, found exclusively in GeForce RTX graphics cards (from the RTX 20 Series onwards, including the RTX 30, 40, and upcoming series), to render games at a lower internal resolution and then intelligently upscale them to a higher target resolution. This sophisticated process dramatically boosts frame rates while often delivering image quality comparable to, or even better than, native rendering. DLSS 4.5 represents the second generation of NVIDIA's transformer model, bringing further refinements to image reconstruction, temporal stability, and overall visual fidelity, making it an incredibly powerful tool for gamers. Its advanced AI algorithms are particularly adept at reducing shimmering and artifacting that can plague traditional upscaling methods. Shimmering, often seen as a [flickering](https://ctrlaltstock.com/blog/vrr-flickering-with-nvidia-frame-gen-2026) effect on fine details or distant objects, and artifacting, such as "ghosting" or smearing around moving elements, are significantly minimised by DLSS 4.5's enhanced temporal data processing. This means clearer textures, more stable edges, and a generally cleaner image, even when playing at 4K resolution on a mid-range card like an RTX 3060 Ti, which might struggle with native 4K. DLSS also offers various modes, such as Quality (which typically renders at 66% of the target resolution), Balanced (58%), Performance (50%), and Ultra Performance (33%), allowing users to fine-tune the balance between visual quality and frame rate according to their specific hardware and preferences.
