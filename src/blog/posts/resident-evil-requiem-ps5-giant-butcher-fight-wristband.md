Recent entries like *Resident Evil Village* (launched at £59.99) and *Resident Evil 4 Remake* (launched at £54.99, later standardising at £59.99 for the base edition) set a clear precedent. If Requiem follows this pattern, expect a launch price of £54.99 to £59.99 for the standard digital or physical edition on PlayStation Store and at UK retailers.

Based on the *RE4 Remake* Deluxe Edition (£79.99) and *Village* Gold Edition (£69.99), a hypothetical "Rhodes Hill Clinic Edition" for Requiem could easily command £74.99 to £84.99. Collector's editions with elaborate statues, like those from Prime 1 Studio or Figurama, could push beyond £150, but these are niche imports often requiring customs handling for UK buyers.
Pre-order bonuses and retailer-specific content are almost guaranteed, given Capcom's history. UK retailers like [Amazon UK](https://ctrlaltstock.com/blog/avatar-last-airbender-game-9-99-amazon), GAME, and ShopTo will likely offer exclusive content—perhaps the Rhodes Hill Clinic wristband as a physical replica or exclusive in-game skins for Grace's outfit or the starting pistol. Keep in mind that limited-run horror titles, especially those that capture the community's imagination like this leak suggests, often see price spikes post-launch if physical supply is tight, so early adopters might pay a premium from third-party sellers on eBay or CEX. Digital versions will, of course, remain at RRP.
For now, the best strategy is to watch official Capcom channels (Capcom UK's Twitter/X and YouTube) and join communities like our CtrlAltStock Discord, where members share the latest rumours and pre-order alerts. However, the production values in the leaked video (clean footage, custom UI, bespoke enemy model) suggest a more official origin, making the £54.99-£59.99 prediction the most realistic.

**Resident Evil Village (PS5) – £19.99-£24.99** – Still one of the best PS5 horror experiences. The standard edition frequently drops to £17.99 on the PlayStation Store during seasonal sales, and physical copies at retailers like Argos or Currys can be found for £19.99. The Winters' Expansion (The Mercenaries, Shadows of Rose) is often bundled for £24.99. Great value if you haven't played it yet, and it directly showcases Capcom's current RE Engine prowess on PS5.
**Resident Evil 4 Remake (PS5) – £44.99-£49.99** – A stunning reimagining that proves Capcom knows how to modernise classics. The standard physical edition sits at £49.99 at most retailers, but ShopTo and Amazon UK regularly discount it to £42.99-£44.99. The Deluxe Edition, containing the extra weapon charms and soundtrack, is £69.99 but can be found for £59.99 during Black Friday. Its third-person, more action-heavy combat provides a direct point of comparison to the "Giant Butcher" footage.
**The Dark Pictures Anthology: The Devil in Me (PS5) – £19.99-£24.99** – Supermassive Games' take on horror, with strong PS5 performance (60fps in performance mode) and plenty of jump scares. Often bundled with the previous three games in the anthology for £29.99 on the PlayStation Store. A good contrast to Capcom's style, focusing more on narrative choices and cinematic horror.
**Dead Space Remake (PS5) – £34.99-£39.99** – Not Resident Evil, but scratches that same sci-fi horror itch with incredible atmosphere, dismemberment-focused combat, and a masterclass in audio design. Physical copies at GAME are £34.99, and it frequently appears in EA Play trials or on PlayStation Plus Extra. Its zero-G combat and resource management are a different flavour but equally tense.
**Alan Wake 2 (PS5) – £54.99** – A newer benchmark for psychological horror. While more focused on narrative and exploration than combat, its use of light as a mechanic and its dual-reality structure offer a sophisticated take on the genre. Full price, but a must-play for serious horror aficionados awaiting Requiem.
