**H2: What's the Story? Resident Evil Requiem Takes the PSSR Plunge**

Capcom and Sony have officially confirmed that *Resident Evil Requiem* will be the inaugural title to utilize Sony's brand-new PSSR (Precision Super Sampling Rendering) upscaler tech on the PlayStation 5 Pro. This isn't a minor tweak; it's a fundamental overhaul designed to push the console's graphical capabilities beyond anything previously seen. As Capcom's statement declared, "We hope you will experience this unprecedented level of horror and visual fidelity, and the new gameplay feel it delivers." The implications are staggering.

The core of this innovation lies in PSSR's ability to process textures and details with unparalleled precision. Traditional upscaling methods often struggle with complex, intricate textures found in modern games, leading to visible artifacts or a loss of clarity. PSSR, however, leverages advanced AI and computational power to analyze and render these elements with near-photorealistic accuracy. Sony's own technical breakdown highlights how the upgraded PSSR has allowed developers to "successfully process these details and textural particularities," resulting in environments that feel tangible and alive. Imagine the dense, atmospheric corridors of Raccoon City rendered with such crystal-clear detail that every tear in the wall, every speck of dust, and every subtle lighting effect becomes a visceral part of the horror. This isn't just about higher resolution; it's about a new level of expressive detail that immerses players completely.
