Starfield, Bethesda's monumental space exploration RPG, has been an Xbox and PC exclusive since its launch, leaving PlayStation 5 owners gazing longingly at the stars. But that could all change very soon! Fresh rumours are swirling about an imminent PS5 release, potentially hitting consoles on April 7, 2026, and, even more exciting for bargain hunters, at a launch price reportedly lower than its original Xbox debut. For UK gamers who've been patiently waiting to dive into the vast cosmos, this could be the perfect opportunity to snag a highly anticipated title without having to pay full price, and experience one of the most ambitious new IPs from Bethesda in decades.
From its stunning visuals to its sprawling universe featuring over 1,000 planets, Starfield offers an unparalleled sense of discovery. Players can create their own character, join various factions like the United Colonies or Freestar Collective, build custom spaceships from scratch, establish outposts, and engage in both ground combat and thrilling dogfights in space. The sheer scale and freedom promised by Bethesda – known for beloved franchises like The Elder Scrolls and Fallout – made Starfield a must-play for many, and its absence on PlayStation has been a significant point of contention for console owners not invested in the Xbox ecosystem. The prospect of exploring this vast, handcrafted universe, uncovering ancient alien mysteries, and charting new paths through the stars, all on their preferred console, is undoubtedly a tantalising one.
## What's the story?
For months, the gaming world has been buzzing with whispers about Starfield making the jump to PlayStation 5. Originally launched on September 6, 2023, as a flagship title for Xbox Series X|S and PC, the idea of Bethesda's latest epic becoming multi-platform has been a hot topic of discussion, especially given Microsoft's recent strategy shift regarding some of its previously exclusive titles. This shift, championed by Xbox head Phil Spencer, aims to bring Xbox games to "more players on more platforms," recognising the value of reaching a wider audience and leveraging existing intellectual property. This strategic pivot has already seen several Xbox-exclusive titles make their way to other platforms, including the acclaimed rhythm-action game Hi-Fi Rush, the survival adventure Grounded, and the narrative-driven Pentiment, all of which have found new audiences on PlayStation and Nintendo Switch. Even the popular pirate adventure Sea of Thieves is slated for a PlayStation release, demonstrating a clear pattern in Microsoft's evolving approach to exclusivity. This move is often seen as a way to bolster revenue, enhance brand presence, and potentially even strengthen the Game Pass subscription service by making its offerings available to a broader player base, regardless of their primary console.
Today, those whispers have gained significant traction. A new report from Dealabs, citing the highly reliable leaker billbil-kun, has not only corroborated the long-rumoured PS5 release but has also provided more concrete details. Billbil-kun, known for accurately leaking PlayStation Plus lineups and major game announcements well in advance, suggests that Starfield is indeed heading to PlayStation 5. Furthermore, the report indicates a potential launch date of April 7, 2026, which, while a considerable wait for eager fans, aligns with typical development cycles for porting large-scale RPGs and integrating any potential platform-specific enhancements. This date could also potentially coincide with a major content update or the release of the game's first major expansion, "Shattered Space," which has been confirmed to be in development.
Crucially, the leak also highlights a prospective launch price for the PS5 version that is reportedly lower than its initial Xbox release. When Starfield first launched on Xbox Series X|S and PC, the standard edition retailed for £69.99 in the UK. The Premium Edition, which included early access and the "Shattered Space" expansion upon release, was priced at £99.99. There was also a collector's Constellation Edition, which, while limited, typically sold for around £249.99 and came with physical collectibles like a Steelbook case and a replica Chronomark watch. The rumour suggests that the PlayStation 5 launch price could be set at a more competitive entry point, potentially somewhere in the range of £49.99 to £59.99 for the standard edition. This revised pricing strategy could be a deliberate move to attract a new wave of players who might have been hesitant to pay full price for a game that has already been out for some time, or to simply make it more appealing to a fresh console audience.
For PlayStation owners, this means the opportunity to finally experience Starfield's intricate gameplay loop. Players begin their journey as a miner who discovers a mysterious artefact, leading them to join Constellation, a group of explorers dedicated to uncovering the galaxy's secrets. From there, the universe truly opens up. You can spend hours customising your character with a deep suite of options, then design and build your dream spaceship, managing everything from its weapons systems to its cargo capacity. Exploring planets ranges from dense, handcrafted cities like New Atlantis and Akila City, bustling with quests and characters, to vast, procedurally generated alien landscapes ripe for resource gathering and outpost construction. Combat is a mix of first-person shooting on foot, utilising a wide array of weapons and abilities, and exhilarating space battles where strategic manoeuvring and ship upgrades are key. The game's narrative branches significantly based on player choices, allowing for multiple playthroughs and a truly personalised adventure.
